Storefronts and other useful links

These are some of the places I most commonly visit to buy and research games.

Storefronts:

  • Steam: No introduction needed.
  • Humble Bundle: The bundle quality can vary, but there's good deals for indie gamers here.
  • GOG (formerly Good Old Games): A good place for DRM-free games and the occasional giveaway.
  • itch.io: Lots of smaller indie games here, with the odd breakout hit.
  • Epic Games: Some controversial business practices, but the free weekly games are nice.

Other:

  • Backloggery: Keeps track of video game acquisitions and completions.
  • Backloggd: An alternative to Backloggery. I haven't tried it, but some people swear by it.
  • HowLongToBeat: Used to estimate game completion times, among other things.
  • SteamDB: Tracks price history and regional pricing for Steam games.
  • ProtonDB: Shows Linux and Steam Deck compatibility for Steam games.
